# Introduction and Context

**Background and Context**: In the modern healthcare landscape, the efficient management of patient records, clinical measurements, and professional scheduling is paramount. Traditional paper-based or fragmented digital systems often lead to communication silos and delayed care. The GOsasun project emerged from the need to provide a unified digital gateway that connects all stakeholders in a health center—patients, healthcare workers, and receptionists—minimizing administrative overhead while maximizing the quality of patient-provider interactions through real-time data accessibility.

**Project Objectives**: The primary goal of GOsasun is to implement a secure, scalable healthcare portal that achieves several SMART objectives. These include enabling patients to independently record and monitor their health vitals with immediate visual feedback, providing healthcare workers with a centralized dashboard for appointment scheduling and prescription management, and equipping administrative staff with comprehensive tools for user oversight. Each feature is designed to be specific, measurable, and highly relevant to improving clinical workflows and patient self-care.

**Scope and Expected Outcomes**: The technical scope of the GOsasun platform encompasses a full-stack architecture, utilizing PHP and jQuery for the web interface and C# for the desktop component. A key technical feature is the system's integration with real tensiometers to obtain accurate blood pressure and pulse data via XML exchange. Furthermore, the platform manages weight and height metrics to generate detailed clinical follow-ups and supports the attachment of essential medical documentation. The expected outcomes include a significant reduction in scheduling conflicts, improved patient adherence to monitoring protocols through automated alerts, and a more streamlined administrative process, ultimately fostering a more responsive and patient-centered healthcare environment.
